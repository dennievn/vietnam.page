VIETNAM

Home of the Independence, Freedom, and Happiness.

Vietnam, located on the Indochina Peninsula in Southeast Asia, is one of the most populated and also the most diversed country on Earth. Home to a variety of ethnic groups, and containing 16% of the world's species, it is expected to rise as the 21st largest economy by 2025.

The History..
  • 1 BCE: Viet people in north form agricultural and bronze-working kingdoms at Dongson in Red River Valley.
  • 208 BCE: Trieu Da, a Chinese general conquers Viet king Au Lac. He builds a capital and makes himself emperor of the southern land, “Nam Viet,” which includes part of present northern Vietnam and China.
  • 111 BCE: Han Chinese emperor Wu conquers Viet people, beginning nearly 1,000 years of Chinese rule in the north. What is now southern Vietnam was occupied by Hindu Cham kingdoms, related linguistically to Malays.
  • 40 CE: Two Trung sisters lead rebellion against Chinese. After regaining rule, China calls the area “Annam,” the pacified south. Capital moved near to present capital Hanoi.
  • 939 CE: Chinese overthrown and Viet kingdom re-established; now called Dai Viet (Great Viet) under Emperor Dinh Bo Linh.
  • 1000s-1600s: Confucian and Mahayana Buddhist Viets begin long period of expansion south into Hindu Cham territories.
  • 1428-1789: Le Dynasty in power.
  • 1500s: Intermittent civil wars.
  • 1613: Civil war between northen and southern Dai Viet.
  • 1627: Alexandre de Rhodes, a French missionary, adapts Roman alphabet to transcribe Vietnamese language.
  • 1802: Nguyen Dynasty established by Emperor Gia Long, capital moved to Hué. Country now known as Vietnam.
  • 1859: France captures Saigon, extends control to Laos and Cambodia, forming French Indochina.
  • 1927-1930: Two communist parties founded in north to resist French rule, but heavily repressed.
  • 1940: World War II begins. Japan invades Vietnam.
  • 1945: Japanese surrender. Ho Chi Minh declares independence and unification of French colonial provinces as Vietnam.
  • 1946-1954: French-Viet Minh war (also known as First Vietnam War). China and the USSR support Ho Chi Minh; United States supports France as a way to contain the spread of communism in Southeast Asia.
  • 1975: Communists take Saigon. Last remaining United States citizens evacuated. Vietnam unified under Communist rule. Saigon renamed Ho Chi Minh City.
  • 1986: Vietnam commits to “doi moi” (renovation), a program of social and market reforms.
  • 1995: Diplomatic relations normalized between United States and Vietnam.
  • 2007: Vietnam became a member of the WTO.
  • 2010: Ngô Bảo Châu won the 2010 Fields Medal for his proof of fundamental lemma in the theory of automorphic forms.
  • 2013: Programmer Dong Nguyen released mobile game Flappy Bird. It became the most downloaded free game in the US Apple App Stores, and Chinese App Stores. But the app was removed by 2014 dued to its legal issues.
